# Speaker Confirmation Email Drafts: Edge Cases CCS 2026

Below you will find a general template followed by personalized drafts for each of the 14 speakers who submitted to the call for proposals.

---

## Master Email Template

**Subject:** Speaker Confirmation: Edge Cases @ CCS 2026 Satellite Symposium

**Body:**
Dear [Speaker Name],

Thank you very much for submitting your proposal to the **Edge Cases: Surprising and Unconventional Applications in Network Science** satellite symposium at CCS 2026 in Binghamton. 

We found your topic, *"[Proposed Presentation Title]"*, to be a fantastic fit for the symposium's goal of showcasing unconventional applications of network science. We are writing to formally lock in your confirmation to present.

The symposium is scheduled for **Wednesday, October 14, from 2:30 PM to 5:45 PM** in **Lecture Hall 10** at Binghamton University. We plan to run a high-density, engaging session, and are allocating **15-minute presentation slots** (including Q&A) for general talks.

To help us finalize the schedule and populate the official website, could you please confirm the following by **[Insert Deadline, e.g., June 30th]**:

1. **Confirmation of Attendance:** Will you be presenting in person at Binghamton, or do you need to present remotely? (Please note that we are unfortunately unable to offer financial support for travel).
2. **Talk Details:** Let us know if you would like to update your talk title or if the original title is good to go.
3. **Bio/Blurb:** A short blurb or abstract of your talk (approx. 100–150 words) if you'd like to refine the one you submitted.
4. **Website Assets:** 
   - Your preferred institutional affiliation.
   - A link to your personal website or academic profile.
   - A profile photo (preferably square, high-resolution) for your speaker card on the website.

If you have any questions, please let us know! We look forward to hearing back from you.

Best regards,

Phileas Dazeley-Gaist
*Edge Cases Organizing Committee*
